Movies review

Each of us has faced the problem of choosing a movie for the evening. Among the multitude of films, it's not always easy to find a quality one. That's why I want to share with you my list of movies worth watching. Let's start by defining the genre. Below, you can find a list of the most popular genres:

  • Drama
  • Detective
  • Comedies
  • Horror
  • Science Fiction
  • Historical
  • Thriller
  • Musical

Half of the work is done. After you've familiarized yourself with the list of genres and what they mean, you can take a look at my recommendations based on the genre.

Drama Detective Comedies Science Fiction Historical

Drama

  1. The Green Mile

    This film is a true classic that will touch your heart. The Green Mile weaves an unforgettable narrative set against the backdrop of a Southern prison. Tom Hanks delivers an outstanding performance, and the film's exploration of good and evil is thought-provoking and deeply moving.

    See trailer

  2. Forest Gump

    Forrest Gump is a masterclass in storytelling. The film's unique perspective on historical events, coupled with Tom Hanks' endearing performance, creates a truly iconic character. It's a movie that reminds us of the beauty of simplicity and the power of kindness

    See trailer

  3. Knockin' on Heaven's Door

    Knockin' on Heaven's Door is a poignant and unique film that beautifully blends dark comedy and drama. The story of two unlikely friends, each facing their mortality, embarking on a journey to fulfill their final wishes is both heartwarming and thought-provoking. The film's title, borrowing from the iconic Bob Dylan song, sets the tone for a tale that explores the complexities of life, death, and the pursuit of happiness.

    See trailer

  4. The Truman Show

    The Truman Show is a thought-provoking and entertaining movie that remains relevant in the age of reality TV and social media. It challenges us to question the nature of our own reality and the influence of media on our lives. The film is a testament to the power of storytelling and the search for genuine human connection in an increasingly mediated world.

    See trailer

Detective

  1. The Hateful Eight

    The Hateful Eight is a dark and violent tale that explores themes of betrayal, justice, and the blurred lines between right and wrong. It is a visually stunning film with a memorable score by Ennio Morricone. Tarantino's craftsmanship in creating tension and intrigue is on full display, making it a compelling addition to his filmography.

    See trailer

  2. Seven

    Brad Pitt and Morgan Freeman deliver powerful performances as the two detectives, and Kevin Spacey is chillingly effective as the enigmatic and sadistic serial killer. The film's exploration of themes related to sin, morality, and the consequences of human actions has made it a cult classic in the thriller genre.

    See trailer

  3. The Lincoln Lawyer

    The film is praised for its engaging plot, strong performances, and McConaughey's charismatic portrayal of the lead character. "The Lincoln Lawyer" offers a mix of legal drama, suspense, and intrigue, making it an entertaining and well-executed courtroom thriller. If you enjoy legal thrillers with a touch of moral ambiguity and a charismatic lead, "The Lincoln Lawyer" is worth a watch. It showcases the complexities of the legal system and the gray areas that attorneys often navigate in their pursuit of justice.

    See trailer

  4. Murder on the Orient Express

    The film is an adaptation of Agatha Christie's classic novel of the same name. The story revolves around a murder that occurs on a luxurious train, the Orient Express, during a journey from Istanbul to Calais. Poirot, who happens to be a passenger on the train, is asked to solve the murder mystery.

    See trailer

Comedies

  1. Green Book

    The film is a heartwarming and thought-provoking story that sheds light on the issues of racial inequality and discrimination while also celebrating the transformative power of human connection. "Green Book" is a compelling and highly recommended film for those who appreciate stories of friendship and social change.

    See trailer

  2. The Gentlemen

    Guy Ritchie's signature style, which often involves clever storytelling and colorful characters, is on full display in "The Gentlemen." The film combines elements of crime, dark comedy, and suspense, creating an engaging and entertaining experience. If you enjoy films with a blend of humor, intrigue, and memorable characters, "The Gentlemen" is a stylish and entertaining choice. It's a clever and well-executed crime comedy that keeps the audience guessing until the very end.

    See trailer

  3. The Hangover

    "The Hangover" is a 2009 American comedy film directed by Todd Phillips. The film follows the misadventures of a group of friends who travel to Las Vegas for a bachelor party and wake up the next morning with no memory of the previous night and the groom-to-be missing.

    See trailer

Science Fiction

  1. Interstellar

    The story is set in a near-future Earth facing environmental collapse, and humanity's survival is at stake. McConaughey's character, Joseph Cooper, a former NASA pilot, is recruited for a mission to explore new habitable worlds through a wormhole near Saturn. The film explores complex scientific concepts, space exploration, and the impact of time dilation on human relationships.

    See trailer

  2. Inception

    If you enjoy intellectually stimulating and visually captivating films, "Inception" is a must-watch. It's a cinematic journey that invites audiences to explore the depths of the mind and the power of ideas.

    See trailer

  3. The Martian

    The film's balance of science and drama, along with its thrilling storyline, makes it a compelling choice for fans of science fiction and survival stories. "The Martian" is a captivating and well-executed film that celebrates human perseverance in the face of overwhelming odds.

    See trailer

Historical

  1. Operation Finale

    If you are interested in historical dramas that shed light on important events and ethical questions related to World War II and the Holocaust, "Operation Finale" offers a gripping and thought-provoking narrative. It showcases the lengths to which individuals and nations will go to seek justice for war crimes and the haunting legacy of the Holocaust.

    See trailer

  2. Narvik

    This is a war movie but also a movie about relationships in wartime. It's good to know about the circumstances that lead to the Battle of Narvik in 1940.This film like other worthwhile war movies before it, clearly brings to the fore the ugliness of war. Man has yet to find a better way to settle differences and to rein in his impulse to dominate and subjugate others.

    See trailer

  3. Against the Ice

    In the end, the most remarkable thing about Against the Ice is that a real-life story of two men at the mercy of the unforgiving elements, of hunger and illness, possible attack and encroaching madness, can be so curiously deprived of tension.

    See trailer
